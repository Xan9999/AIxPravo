# US Constitution - Quick Reference Guide

**Source:** National Archives (archives.gov) - Public Domain  
**Purpose:** Legal reference for chatbot context

---

## Document Structure

The Constitution is chunked into logical sections for optimal retrieval and citation:

### Core Articles

1. **Preamble** - Purpose and goals of the Constitution
2. **Article I** - Legislative Branch (Congress)
   - Section 1: Legislative powers vested in Congress
   - Section 8: Enumerated powers of Congress
3. **Article II** - Executive Branch (President)
   - Section 1: Executive power and qualifications
   - Section 2: Presidential powers (Commander in Chief, treaties, appointments)
4. **Article III** - Judicial Branch (Courts)
   - Section 1: Judicial power and judge tenure
   - Section 2: Scope of federal judicial power

### Bill of Rights (Amendments 1-10)

- **Amendment I** - Freedom of religion, speech, press, assembly, petition
- **Amendment II** - Right to bear arms
- **Amendment III** - No quartering of soldiers
- **Amendment IV** - Protection against unreasonable search and seizure
- **Amendment V** - Due process, self-incrimination, double jeopardy, eminent domain
- **Amendment VI** - Right to fair and speedy trial
- **Amendment VII** - Right to jury trial in civil cases
- **Amendment VIII** - Protection against cruel and unusual punishment
- **Amendment IX** - Rights retained by the people
- **Amendment X** - Powers reserved to states and people

### Reconstruction Amendments (11-15)

- **Amendment XI** (1795) - Suits against states
- **Amendment XII** (1804) - Electoral college procedures
- **Amendment XIII** (1865) - Abolition of slavery
- **Amendment XIV** (1868) - Equal protection, due process, citizenship
- **Amendment XV** (1870) - Voting rights regardless of race

### Progressive Era Amendments (16-19)

- **Amendment XVI** (1913) - Income tax
- **Amendment XVII** (1913) - Direct election of senators
- **Amendment XVIII** (1919) - Prohibition (repealed by 21st)
- **Amendment XIX** (1920) - Women's suffrage

### Modern Amendments (20-27)

- **Amendment XX** (1933) - Presidential term and succession ("Lame Duck")
- **Amendment XXI** (1933) - Repeal of prohibition
- **Amendment XXII** (1951) - Presidential term limits (two terms)
- **Amendment XXIII** (1961) - DC electoral votes
- **Amendment XXIV** (1964) - Poll tax prohibition
- **Amendment XXV** (1967) - Presidential succession and disability
- **Amendment XXVI** (1971) - Voting age lowered to 18
- **Amendment XXVII** (1992) - Congressional compensation

## Citation Format

Each chunk includes:
- **ID**: Unique identifier (e.g., `const_amend1`)
- **Title**: Descriptive name with amendment/article number
- **Content**: Exact constitutional text
- **Source**: Specific citation (e.g., "US Constitution, Amendment I")
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal

## Usage in Chatbot

The chatbot will:
1. Parse user questions to identify relevant constitutional topics
2. Retrieve the most relevant chunks (typically 3)
3. Use AI to reason through the constitutional text
4. Provide answers with specific citations
5. Include confidence scores based on relevance
